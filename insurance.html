Accepted Insurance

We Accept Most Major Insurance Plans

Our billing team has decades of experience navigating insurance requirements — we handle everything on your behalf.

MC
Medicare Part B

Medicare Part B covers durable medical equipment (DME) that is medically necessary for use in your home. This is the most common coverage type for our patients.

  • Manual & power wheelchairs
  • Mobility scooters (power-operated vehicles)
  • Walkers and rollators
  • Lift chair mechanisms
  • 20% patient coinsurance after deductible
MD
Medicaid

Medicaid programs in most states cover a broader range of mobility equipment, often with little or no out-of-pocket cost for eligible beneficiaries.

  • Often covers full equipment cost
  • Full lift chair coverage (state-dependent)
  • Home modifications & ramps
  • Complex rehab technology
  • Low or zero patient responsibility
PPO
Private PPO & HMO Plans

Most private insurance plans include DME benefits. We are in-network with major carriers and work with out-of-network insurers as well.

  • Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealth
  • Blue Cross Blue Shield
  • Humana & Molina
  • Veterans Affairs (VA)
  • Workers' Compensation
$$
Self-Pay & Financing

No insurance? No problem. We offer competitive self-pay pricing and flexible financing options to make equipment accessible to everyone.

  • Transparent self-pay pricing
  • 0% financing available (OAC)
  • Rental options for short-term needs
  • Rent-to-own programs
  • Flexible monthly payment plans
Qualification Process

How to Qualify for Your Equipment

Most patients qualify for coverage without even knowing it. Here's how the process works from start to finish.

1

Contact Us to Start

Call us or fill out our eligibility form below. We'll collect your insurance information, Medicare/Medicaid number, and details about your mobility limitations.

2

Free Insurance Verification

Our billing team contacts your insurance plan to verify your benefits, confirm we are in-network, and determine your expected out-of-pocket cost before you commit to anything.

3

Physician Coordination

We work directly with your doctor to obtain the required prescription, face-to-face examination documentation, and written order. We provide the paperwork — your doctor signs.

4

Clinical Assessment (if required)

For complex equipment like power chairs, our licensed ATP may conduct an in-home or in-office assessment to document your clinical need according to Medicare LCD criteria.

5

Prior Authorization & Approval

We submit all documentation to your insurer for prior authorization. We monitor the status and follow up until approval is received — typically 5–14 business days.

6

Delivery, Fitting & Training

Once approved, we schedule delivery at your convenience. Our specialist delivers, fits, and trains you on your equipment — and bills your insurance directly.

What You'll Need

Required Documentation

Having these documents ready speeds up the approval process significantly.

Insurance card (Medicare/Medicaid/private insurance)
Government-issued photo ID
Physician's written order / prescription
Face-to-face examination notes (for power mobility)
Medical records documenting diagnosis & functional limitations
Proof of residence / home address
Don't worry if you're missing documents. We can often help you obtain required documentation by coordinating with your physician and insurance company.
